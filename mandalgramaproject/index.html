About Kuppam

Kuppam is a town located in the Andhra Pradesh, India, in the Chittoor District under Kuppam Mandal. Geographically, it lies at coordinates 12.75°N latitude and 78.37°E longitude, with an average elevation of about 667 meters above sea level. The town is governed by a Municipal Council and administered by the Kuppam Area Development Authority. Kuppam covers an area of approximately 3.10 square kilometers and, according to the 2011 census, has a population of 21,963, resulting in a population density of around 7,080 people per square kilometer. Telugu is the official language, while Telugu, Tamil, and Kannada are commonly spoken regional languages. The town follows Indian Standard Time (UTC+5:30), with the postal PIN code 517425, telephone code +91–08570, and vehicle registration codes AP-39 and AP-03. Kuppam is known for its granite quarries and factories. A granite variety, Kuppam Green, Kuppam has the presence of hydro Extrusion India Pvt Ltd. Hydro is a major aluminium extrusions company headquartered in Oslo, Norway.[8] This has now been acquired by Hindalco Industries Ltd. It is widely known for its granite industry, especially the export-quality “Kuppam Green” stone. The town enjoys good connectivity to Bangalore, about 115–125 km away, with trains being a preferred mode of transport.

Famous in Kuppam

Granite Industry

Kuppam is widely known for its export-quality “Kuppam Green” granite, which is in high demand in international markets.

Tri-State Location

The town lies near the borders of Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, and Tamil Nadu, making it an important regional junction.

Industrial Presence

Kuppam hosts major industries like aluminium extrusion units, contributing to local employment and development.
